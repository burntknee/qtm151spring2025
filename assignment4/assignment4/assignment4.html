    • Create at three histograms: one with a small sample size (e.g., 10), one with a medium sample size (e.g., 100), and one with a large sample size (e.g., 1000).

In[22]:
num_simulations = 250
sample_size_list = [10, 100, 1000]

for sample_size in sample_size_list:
    vec_xbar = [None] * num_simulations
    

    for iteration in range(num_simulations):
        vec_unif  = np.random.uniform(low = -2, high=2, size = sample_size)
        vec_xbar[iteration] = vec_unif.mean()
        
    plt.hist(vec_xbar)
    plt.title("Distribution of Xbar when n is " + str(sample_size))
    plt.ylabel("Frequency")
    plt.xlabel("Values of Xbar")
    plt.show()

Write your answer in this cell

A small sample size reduces the accuracy to the true mean; the sample means are more spread out. Comparatively, a medium sample size has sample means increasingly approaching the true mean and less variability. A large sample size continues this trend, with the least variability and most accuracy to the true mean. The number of simulations affects the smoothness of the histogram; with more simulations the histogram is more smooth and the opposite also holds true.
